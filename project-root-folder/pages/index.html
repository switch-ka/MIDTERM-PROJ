What is Global Warming

Global warming is the phenomenon of increasing average air temperatures near the surface of Earth over the past one to two centuries. Climate scientists have gathered detailed observations of various weather phenomena (such as temperatures, precipitation, and storms) and of related influences on climate (such as ocean currents and the atmosphere’s chemical composition). These data indicate that Earth’s climate has changed over almost every conceivable timescale since the beginning of geologic time, and that human activities since at least the beginning of the Industrial Revolution have a growing influence over the pace and extent of present-day climate change.

Countries Most Affected by Global Warming

1. Chad

It is considered the most vulnerable country on the planet according to the University of Notre Dame's country vulnerability studies due to flooding, increased military confrontation, and famine affecting the whole country.

2. Somalia

As a result of both drought and political challenges within the nation, the International Rescue Committee (IRC) anticipates that over eight million Somalis may face the threat of famine throughout the year.

3. Syria

Following ten years of conflict, a combination of war, drought, and a February 2023 earthquake has positioned the Middle Eastern country as one of the nations facing significant challenges in coping with climate-related shocks.
